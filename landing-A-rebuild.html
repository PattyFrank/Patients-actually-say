Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the Patient Sentiment Report, and who is it for?
It's a 2026 research report from PatientPartner analysing 7,823 mentor–patient conversations across six therapy areas. It's built for pharma marketing, patient support, and medical affairs leaders who want to understand what patients say when they feel safe enough to be honest — and what that means for adherence and program ROI.
2. How does PatientPartner ensure compliance with HIPAA and GDPR?
All conversation data in this report is de-identified per HIPAA Safe Harbor with IRB exemption on file. PatientPartner is HIPAA-compliant, SOC 2 certified, and built for regulated pharma and med-device environments.
3. What kind of measurable ROI can we expect from peer mentorship?
Patients connected with a mentor begin treatment 69 days sooner on average (28 days vs. 97 days unconnected) and are 68% more likely to initiate. Most of our clients see payback inside the first 6 months from accelerated initiation alone — before counting downstream adherence gains.
4. How does the platform support clinical trial recruitment and retention?
The same mentor-matching infrastructure that supports commercial PSPs is used for trial recruitment and onboarding. Mentors who've completed similar treatments help reduce screen-fail rates and improve retention across enrollment windows.
5. What are the core features of the PatientPartner platform?
Mentor-matching engine, conversation infrastructure, sentiment analytics dashboard, HIPAA-compliant intake flow, and outcomes reporting — all integrated with your existing PSP and CRM.
6. Can PatientPartner integrate with our existing systems (CRM, EHR, portals)?
Yes. We integrate with HubSpot, Salesforce, Veeva, and most major PSP platforms via API or secure SFTP. Implementation typically takes 4–6 weeks end-to-end.
7. Who are the mentors, and how are they vetted and matched?
Mentors are real patients who've been through similar treatment journeys. Each is recruited, trained on regulatory guardrails, and matched to patients based on therapy area, demographics, and the specific fears or questions surfaced in intake.
